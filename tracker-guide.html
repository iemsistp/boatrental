Cold start (first time starting the boat)
  1. Release the latch and bring the motor down until the head is in the water (or all the way, if possible).
  2. Turn the red switch to run position, push the key in to choke, and start.
  3. Always make sure water is coming out from the pee hole (line of water out the side of the motor).

Once the engine is warm, just turn the key to start — this is for a cold start only.

Boat equipment
Trolling motor
Just plug in at the front and use.
Fish finder
Do not leave it on if you're not using it — the battery will drain.
Bilge pump
Prevents flooding caused by unexpected leaks or rain.
Life jackets
4 life jackets for regulation and safety equipment are stored on the boat.
Rear seat
Be careful — if the seat is not turned properly, you can crack the motor cover.
Docking
4-5 bumpers and 2 ropes are provided for docking.
Ownership documents
A copy of ownership is stored under the driver seat.
Returning the boat
  1. Make sure the boat is secured with latches and tie downs.
  2. Store all belongings and equipment so nothing flies away.
  3. Any lost equipment or damage to the boat will be charged.
  4. Refill the boat with premium gas and keep your receipt — proof is required when returned.
